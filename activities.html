WHERE TO GO

Cartagena

Known as the ‘Jewel of the Caribbean’, Cartagena is almost too beautiful for words. Year-round sunny skies, clear Caribbean waters, colorful colonial architecture and a wealth of Afro-Colombian culture to explore. A vast of cobblestone laneways that weave between pastel-painted walls, plants that flow across balcony alcoves and a ocean that sparkles in the horizon. The whole thing feels like wandering through a romantic film-set.
~ Available to all ages

La Zona Cafetera

Aslo known as 'the Coffee Triangle', this region has a booming coffee production business and the verdant hill country in which the crop is produced is exceptionally scenic. Manizales, Salento and Armenia are the principal production towns. Aside from being scenic towns in their own rights, the surrounding rolling hills and gullies, coated in lush greenery year-round, make for a very attractive backdrop as if it were from a Windows home screen.
~ Available to all ages

Carribean Beaches

North of Santa Marta you will come across Tayrona National Park, an outlandishly gorgeous tract of dense tropical rainforest, hidden coves, golden sands and clear waters. In fact, any of the beaches along Colombia’s northern coast are pretty special. Baru, Islas de Rosario, Cabo San Juan being the other few top picks from tourists. Turquoise waters, pearl white sand, and bright orange desert in the background, literally a beach lovers' dream, who wouldn't want to step foot here? Just make sure to come with that special someone, get cozy under the sun.
~ Available to all ages

Villa de Leyva

Almost every place in Colombia has scenic greenery, but the colonial white-washed town feels like stepping into the past. Built by soldiers who had come to conquer the New World, they had to go big or go home. As a result, they were left with luscious green backdrops, chalk white buildings, with no modern architecture in sight. Considered one of the most impressive and finest towns in all of South America
~ Available to all ages

Barranquilla

Best known for hosting one of the largest parties in Colombia, the city of Barranquilla knows how to run the show with their annual "The Barranquilla Carnival". International artists, parades, adventure tourism, sailing and other numerous cutural activites. Theres almost no way to ever find yourself bored here, theres almost everything to make your trip an unforgettable experience.
~ Available to all ages
